Voice Evaluation Study

Thank you for taking part in this study. You are helping evaluate text-to-speech (TTS) voices designed for virtual assistant applications. Your responses will form part of a dissertation project at the University of Edinburgh investigating how different training strategies affect the perceived quality and speaker identity of synthetic speech.

The study takes approximately 15–20 minutes. All responses are anonymous. There are no right or wrong answers — just rate what you hear.

What you will do

Section 1  Listen to groups of four short clips and rate each clip on friendliness and suitability as a virtual assistant voice (1–5 scale). You will do this for 10 sentences.

Section 2a  Listen to pairs of clips and judge whether each clip sounds more like a virtual assistant or an audiobook narrator. You will do this for 10 sentences.

Section 2b  Listen to 10 pairs of clips and decide whether the two clips in each pair sound like they were produced by different speakers or the same speaker.

Tips

🎧 Please use headphones or earphones if possible. Find a quiet environment and listen to each clip at least once before rating.

⚠  Important — do not judge naturalness. The clips are computer-generated and may sound robotic or imperfect. This is expected and is not what you are being asked to evaluate. Focus only on the specific quality asked about in each section.
